About this hub

The hub is designed as a living, structured view of the Saudi-centered space opportunity landscape, complementing official portals with clearer tagging, filters, and basic analytics.

Why this hub matters

Saudi Arabia’s space sector is undergoing a major acceleration under Vision 2030, with the national space market projected to grow from about USD 8.7 billion in 2024 to around USD 31.6 billion by 2035 and more than USD 2.1 billion already committed in public funding for space programs and commercial enablers such as Neo Space Group.

Institutions like the Saudi Space Agency, the Communications, Space & Technology Commission, the Supreme Space Council, and the Public Investment Fund now form a coordinated architecture that can generate frequent calls for proposals, accelerators, and partnership tracks that are difficult to track in one place.

This hub aggregates and tags those opportunities so founders, researchers, and investors can see the evolving pipeline at a glance, instead of relying on scattered press releases and ministry portals.

Who it serves

  • Startups & SMEs looking for grants, tenders, accelerators, equity, and soft loans matched to downstream apps, EO, satcom, and launch-adjacent manufacturing.
  • Public agencies & ministries that want to broadcast problem statements and competitions like SpaceUp to attract solution providers and system integrators.
  • Universities & research labs mapping scholarships, research grants, and training programs such as RDIA calls, the Saudi Space Academy, and space-focused scholarship tracks.
  • Investors seeking an overview of sovereign funds, VC platforms, and PIF-backed vehicles that can co-invest in space-relevant deals.

Gaps it addresses

  • Fragmented announcement channels across CST, SSA, Monsha’at, RDIA, KAUST, Misk, PIF entities, and international agencies.
  • Inconsistent tracking of open/closed status and recurring deadlines for flagship programs and competitions.
  • Ambiguity around who is eligible for global programs that require European residency, subsidiaries, or other local presence to access funding.
  • Limited transparency on typical ticket sizes for some grants and blended finance mechanisms, making it hard to prioritise applications.
  • Language and tagging gaps, where key schemes are described only in Arabic and seldom classified under space-specific sub-sectors such as EO, satcom, downstream apps, launch, and AI+space.

Methodology & Data Sources

This hub curates public information about space-related grants, tenders, accelerators, and programs relevant to Saudi actors. Records are drawn from:

  • Official Saudi sources (CST, Saudi Space Agency, RDIA, Monsha'at, Misk).
  • GCC partners (UAE Space Agency, MBRSC, Oman's OSAP, regional accelerators).
  • Global portals (ESA, EU Funding & Tenders, NASA, international accelerators and funds).
  • Manual research and periodic updates; this is not an official CST or government website.

Dataset updated periodically; always verify details and check the official link before applying.
